Quasifuga in B♭

This was my first attempt at a fugue. It largely adheres to the conventions of the form, although the construction of the countersubjects is on occasion somewhat removed from the stepwise motion typical of fugal counterpoint.

The subject is lifted from my piano concerto in A Minor, an abandoned mess with few redeeming features, yet containing a few aesthetically pleasing themes. Much like my early compositional efforts, this piece should not be taken particularly seriously.
