Osteomyelitis

Classification by Cierny-Mader Staging System for Osteomyelitis

This classification is based on the injection portion of the bone, the physiological status of the host, and the local environment of the infected bone. (See Table A) (Page 352 textbook)

Stage 1: Medullary Osteomyeletis

Confined to the intermedullary confines of the bone.

Stage 2: Superficial Osteomyelitis

Most often originated from the direct innoculation or contiguous focus of infection. Involves cortical bone.

Stage 3: Localized Osteomyelitis

Usually involves both cortical and medullary bone.

Stage 4: Diffuse Osteomyelitis

A through-and-through process, including those infections with a loss of bony stability either before or after debridement surgery.
