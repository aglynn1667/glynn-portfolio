Cellulitis Syndromes

Erysipelas

A distinctive type of superficial cellulitis of the skin.

Etiology

Predisposing factors:

  • Skin barrier disruption due to trauma
  • Eczema
  • Psoriasis
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes

Microorganisms

Most common cause is group A Streptococcus pyogens.

Less commonnly, S. aureus.

Diagnosis

Clinical:

  • Skin edema
  • Redness
  • Erythema

Patient may have associated:

  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Headache
  • Weakness

Treatment

Empiric treatment to cover S. pyogens and S. aureus.
