Cutaneous Anthrax

B. anthracis infection introduced through skin cuts/abrasions.

Causes

  • Handling infected animals/animal products

  • Typical incubation period is 5â€“7 days

Diagnosis

  • Culture lesions

  • Location: face, neck, hands, arms

  • Development of lesions:

    • Start as painless and pruritic

    • Progress to vesicle, which eventually erupts

    • Finally, painless necrotic ulcer with black eschar

  • May have systemic symptoms of infection

Treatment

  • Non-systemic infection (does not include head/neck lesions)

  • PCN V 500mg QID

  • Ciprofloxacin 500 mg every 12 hours, doxycycline 100mg Q 12, Levofloxacin 750mg Q24 hours, moxifloxacin 400mg Q24 hours for 7-10 days duration
