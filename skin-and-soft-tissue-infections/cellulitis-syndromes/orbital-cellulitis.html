Cellulitis Syndromes

Orbital Cellulitis

Etiology

Most common origin is rhinosinusitis.

Other causes:

  • Orbital trauma
  • Orbital surgery
  • Dacrocystitis
  • Infection of the teeth, middle ear, or face
  • Periorbital anesthesia

Microbiology

Most common pathogens:

  • Staphylococcus
  • Streptococcus

Others:

  • Anaerobes
  • Hemophilus influenza

Rare causes:

  • Acinetobacter species
  • Nocardia brasiliensis
  • Bacillus anthracis
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Neisseria gonorrhoeae
  • Proteus species
  • Pasteurella multocida
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Trichophyton spp.

Mucormycosis should be suspected in immunosuppressed patients.

Diagnosis

Blood cultures, sinus cultures.

CT scan of orbit and sinuses.

MRI orbit and sinuses.

If cavernous sinus thrombosis is suspected, MRI venography or CT venography is recommended.

Treatment

Uncomplicated orbital cellulitis—antibiotics alone.

Ophthalmologist consult.

Otolaryngologist consult.

Prompt treatment with antibiotics:

  • Initial empiric therapy should cover MRSA, S. pneumoniae, other strep species.

  • If intracranial extension is suspected, anerobic coverage should be included.

IV Vancomycin plus IV ceftriaxone/IV cefotaxime.

  • If intracranial extension orbits due to an odontogenic source, add Metronidazole.

  • Once symptoms improve, switch to oral antibiotics (as mentioned in treatment of preseptal cellulitis).

Drainage of abscess or sinusitis.

Duration of therapy

2–3 weeks.
