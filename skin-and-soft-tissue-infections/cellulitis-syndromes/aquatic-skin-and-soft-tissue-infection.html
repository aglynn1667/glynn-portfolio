Aquatic Skin and Soft Tissue Infection

Caused by traumatic injury in combination with water exposure.

For immunocompromised patients, also consider Mycobacterium manum, which grow in low temperature. [1]

Diagnosis

Imaging is not required for superficial wound infections, but it is indicated for deep soft tissue infections.

Aquaculture cellulitis

Table 2. Types of aquaculture cellulitis.

Shellfish, crustaceans, coastal sediment, plankton Vibrio parahaemolyticus 15--17h Foodborne Doxycycline, fluoroquinolone, macrolide
Oysters, seafood, fresh and brackish water Aeromonas hydrophila Unknown Waterborne Trim-sulfa, 2nd & 3rd gen cephalosporins, fluoroquinolones (watch for resistance)
Tilapia, other finfish Streptococcus iniae Unknown Contact Penicillin, amoxicillin plusclavulanic acid
Fresh and marine water Edwardsiella tarda Unknown Foodborne and contact Tetracycline, 3rd gen cephalosporins

Abx duration is for at least five days, longer if no improvement is noticed.[3]

Wound care is recommended for all degrees of skin infection.
