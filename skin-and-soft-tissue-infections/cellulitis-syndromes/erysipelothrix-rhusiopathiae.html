Cellulitis Syndromes

Erysipelothrix rhusiopathiae

  • Gram-positive bacillus, non–spore-forming.

  • May cause soft tissue infection or serious systemic infection.

  • Erysipeloid of Rosenbach.

Risk factors

  • Highest infections seen in swine farmers

  • Can be seen in those handling domestic and marine animals

  • Open wounds/abrasions on hands

Diagnosis/symptoms

  • Cellulitis involving finger/hand

  • 2–7 day incubation period

  • Pain, itching, burning, tingling at infection site, stiffness of finger

  • Small red macule at the inoculation site

  • Develops to a well-developed violaceous lesion with central clearing and a raised border

  • Diagnosis by H&P

Treatment

  • May resolve spontaneously in 3 weeks

  • PCN V 500mg Q6hours or amoxicillin 500mg Q8 hours, 5-10 days or ciprofloxacin 250 mg Q 12 hours or clindamycin 300 mg Q 8 hours

  • Typically resistant to vancomycin and sulfas
