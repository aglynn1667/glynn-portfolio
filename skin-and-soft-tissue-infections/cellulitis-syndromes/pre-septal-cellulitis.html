Cellulitis Syndromes

Pre-septal Cellulitis

Infection of the anterior portion of the eyelid without involvement of the orbit or ocular structures.

Etiology

Most common cause is spread from an adjacent sinusitis, particularly the ethmoid sinusitis. Other causes:

  • Local trauma
  • Insect bites
  • Animal bites
  • Foreign bodies

Microbiology

Typical causes:

  • Staphylococcus
  • Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Other streptococcus species
  • Anaerobes
  • Hemophilus influenza

Rare causes:

  • Acinetobacter species
  • Nocardia brasiliensis
  • Bacillus anthracis
  • Pseudomonas aeruginosa
  • Neisseria gonorrhoeae
  • Proteus species
  • Pasteurella multocida
  • Mycobacterium tuberculosis
  • Trichophyton spp.

Clinical signs

  • Unilateral eyelid swelling and erythema
  • Absence of eye pain
  • Pain with eye movement
  • Visual impairment
  • Proptosis
  • In severe cases, possible chemosis

Diagnosis

CT scan of orbit and sinuses specially to differentiate between preseptal and orbital cellulitis.

Treatment

PO TMP-SMX or PO clindamycin or Doxycycline plus amoxicillin/amoxiclav/ cefpodoxime/cefdinir.
