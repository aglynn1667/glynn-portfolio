Cellulitis Syndromes

Extremities Cellulitis

Lower extremity cellulitis

Likely organism/source

Non-Group A Beta Hemolytic Streptococcus (Groups B, C, G and F streptococci)—infections result from the presence of a venous or a lymphatic compromise.

Staphylococcus aureus—usually associated with skin breakdown or trauma or wound.

Source: macerated or fissured interdigital toe spaces. There could be a concomitant T. pedis that needs to be treated first for treatment to be effective.

Empiric therapy

De-escalate empiric antibiotics once culture sensitivities are available.

Parenteral therapy

  • Cefazolin

  • Nafcillin

  • If patient is allergic to penicillin, consider Vancomycin, Linezolid, or Daptomycin.

Oral therapy

  • Cefalexin

  • If patient is allergic to penicillin, consider Clindamycin or Linezolid.
