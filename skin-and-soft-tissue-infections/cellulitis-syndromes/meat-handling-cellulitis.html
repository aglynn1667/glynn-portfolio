Cellulitis Syndromes

Meat Handling Cellulitis

  • Orf virus

    • Ecthyma contagiosum

    • Infectious pustular dermatitis

    • Contagious pustular dermatitis

  • Cutaneous viral infection

  • Causes erythematous nodule

  • Caused by handling sheep and goats

Cause

  • Infected lesion on living or deceased goat

  • Introduced to human through broken skin/abrasion by direct contact

  • Commonly found on hands/fingers

  • Virus present in environment animal was living in

  • Virus present on equipment used to take care of infected animal

  • Can withstand severe temperatures (heat/cold) and live in environment for years

Risk factors

  • Working with sheep/goats
  • Farmers, veterinarians, etc.

Diagnosis

  • H&P and physical exam

  • May need biopsy (shave or punch)

  • Symptoms present 3–7 days after exposure

  • 6 different stages of healing

  • Human-to-human transmission is rare

  • Patients rarely have systemic symptoms

  • May be complicated with bacterial skin infections

Treatment

  • Spontaneous resolution 6–8 weeks

  • May use topical antiseptic to prevent bacterial infection

  • Immunocompromised patients may need further treatment if persistent lesions occur

  • Wear gloves to prevent future infections
