Common Skin and Soft Tissue Infections

Impetigo

Stages of impetigo by type

There are 3 types of impetigo based on:

  • The bacteria that cause them
  • The sores that they form

Each type goes through a series of stages.

Nonbullous

The most common form of impetigo, mainly caused by Staphylococcus aureus. It goes through the following stages:

  • Usually starts with reddish, itchy sores around the mouth and nose.

  • The sores break open, leaving red and irritated skin around them.

  • A brownish-yellow crust forms.

  • When the crusts heal, there are reddish spots that fade and do not leave scars.

Bullous

Almost always caused by Staphylococcus aureus bacteria.

  • Usually forms larger blisters or bullae filled with a clear fluid that may become darker and cloudy.

    • The blisterse start on unbroken skin and are not surrounded by reddish areas.

  • The blisters become limp and clear and then burst open.

  • A yellowish, crusty sore forms over the area where the blisters broke open.

  • The blisters usually leave no scars behind when they heal.

Ecthyma

This more serious infection is much less common. It occasionally occurs when impetigo is not treated.

Ecthyma goes deeper into the skin than the other forms of impetigo and is more severe.

  • The infection forms painful blisters on the skin of the buttocks, thighs, legs, ankles, and feet.

  • The blisters turn into pus-filled sores with a thicker crust.

  • Often, the skin around the sores turns red.

  • Ecthyma sores heal slowly and may leave scars after they heal.
